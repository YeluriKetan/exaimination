13. Plant anatomy is the study of the different parts of a plant and their structures. It helps scientists understand the function and organization of a plant's physical components.
14. Photosynthesis is the process by which plants use sunlight, carbon dioxide, and water to produce glucose and oxygen. Through this process, plants are able to convert energy from the sun into usable forms that sustain life.
15. By studying plants, scientists can discover new medicines from plant compounds. For example, aspirin was originally derived from the bark of a willow tree. Additionally, many traditional medicines and remedies are made from plants and their extracts, making the study of plants crucial in the field of medicine.
