Quiz:
1. What is an ecosystem?
A) A community of living and nonliving components
B) A community of only living organisms
C) A community of only nonliving components
D) A community of only animals
Answer: A

2. Which process allows energy to enter an ecosystem?
A) Decomposition
B) Photosynthesis
C) Respiration
D) Predation
Answer: B

3. What is the role of decomposers in an ecosystem?
A) They produce energy for the ecosystem.
B) They consume energy from other organisms.
C) They recycle nutrients back into the environment.
D) They are responsible for photosynthesis.
Answer: C

4. Which of the following is NOT a nutrient essential for living organisms?
A) Oxygen
B) Carbon
C) Nitrogen
D) Gold
Answer: D

5. Which term describes the transfer of energy from one organism to another?
A) Nutrient cycling
B) Pollution
C) Photosynthesis
D) Food chain
Answer: D

6. What is a potential consequence of human activities on ecosystems?
A) Increased biodiversity
B) Improved nutrient cycling
C) Disruption of energy flow
D) Increased photosynthesis
Answer: C

7. Which of the following is NOT a component of an ecosystem?
A) Air
B) Soil
C) Rocks
D) Cars
Answer: D

8. True or False: Energy flows in one direction through an ecosystem.
Answer: True

9. True or False: Nutrients are lost from an ecosystem during nutrient cycling.
Answer: False

10. Free-Response: Explain the role of photosynthesis in an ecosystem.
Answer: Photosynthesis is the process by which plants convert sunlight, carbon dioxide, and water into glucose, which is used as energy by the plant. This energy is then passed on to consumers through consumption of plants and is essential for the survival of all living organisms in the ecosystem.
