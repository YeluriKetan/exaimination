Multiple Choice:
1. Which of the following is NOT a key principle of landscape architecture?
A. Incorporating natural features into the design
B. Considering human use and experience
C. Prioritizing economic viability
D. Promoting sustainability

2. Which of the following is NOT a visual aid commonly used in landscape architecture?
A. Drawings
B. Photographs
C. Site maps
D. Blueprints

3. What does sustainability mean in the context of landscape architecture?
A. Creating designs that are environmentally friendly
B. Prioritizing aesthetics over functionality
C. Using only artificial materials in the design
D. Ignoring the natural features of the site

4. Which of the following is NOT a factor that landscape architects consider when designing a space?
A. Topography
B. Economic feasibility
C. Climate
D. Vegetation

5. What role does human experience play in landscape architecture?
A. It is the main focus of landscape architecture.
B. It is not considered in the design process.
C. It is only important in urban settings.
D. It is a key aspect of a well-designed outdoor space.

6. Landscape architecture focuses only on urban settings.
A. True

7. Sustainability is not a concern in landscape architecture.
A. True

8. Diagrams are not commonly used in landscape architecture.
A. True
B. False

9. Landscape architecture is an interdisciplinary field.
A. True
B. False

Free-response:
10. Describe the key principles of landscape architecture.
11. How do landscape architects incorporate natural features into their designs?
12. Explain the role of sustainability in landscape architecture.

Answer Key:
1. C
2. D
3. A
4. B
5. D
6. B
7. B
8. B
9. A
10. The key principles of landscape architecture include incorporating natural features into the design, considering human use and experience, and promoting sustainability.
11. Landscape architects incorporate natural features into their designs by carefully studying the topography, soil conditions, climate, and vegetation of a site and incorporating them into the design in a harmonious way.
12. Sustainability in landscape architecture means creating designs that are environmentally friendly and promote sustainable practices, such as using native plants and implementing green infrastructure.
