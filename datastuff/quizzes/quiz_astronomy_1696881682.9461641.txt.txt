1. Technological advancements have improved our ability to detect and study exoplanets through methods such as direct imaging, spectroscopy, and gravitational lensing. This has allowed us to discover and learn more about a vast number of exoplanets, expanding our understanding of the universe.
2. The discovery of 51 Pegasi b was significant because it was the first confirmed exoplanet and sparked a revolution in the field of astronomy. It paved the way for the discovery of thousands of other exoplanets and has greatly increased our knowledge of planetary systems beyond our own.
3. The transit method involves measuring the dimming of a star's brightness as a planet passes in front of it, while the radial velocity method looks at the slight wobble of a star caused by the gravitational pull of an orbiting planet. Both methods provide information about the size, mass, and orbit of an exoplanet, but they rely on different techniques and have varying levels of accuracy.
