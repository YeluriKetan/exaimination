Free-response question answers:
1. Spiral galaxies are characterized by a flat, rotating disk with arms spiraling outwards. Elliptical galaxies are round or oval in shape and irregular galaxies have no distinct shape.
2. Supermassive black holes play a crucial role in the formation and evolution of galaxies by influencing the growth and structure of the galaxy and impacting the surrounding stars and gas.
3. Technological advancements such as telescopes and space probes have allowed astronomers to observe and photograph galaxies in great detail, providing valuable data and insights into their composition and behavior. They have also helped in detecting and studying supermassive black holes at the center of galaxies.
