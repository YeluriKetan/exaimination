Free Response:
1. A phoneme is a unit of sound in a language, while a grapheme is a unit of writing or symbol that represents a sound.
2. The International Phonetic Alphabet allows linguists to accurately transcribe and compare sounds in different languages.
3. In English, the phonological rule that states plosives (/p/, /t/, /k/) are voiceless at the end of a word can be seen in the difference between the words dog and dock.
4. The study of historical linguistics can help us better understand cultural and societal changes, as well as the origins and relationships between languages. It can also aid in language preservation and revitalization efforts.
