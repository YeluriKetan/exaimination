Subject: History of Art
Page Number: 36
Content: During the Renaissance period, artists began to move away from the traditional religious themes and instead focused on humanism and the celebration of the human form. This shift was influenced by the revival of classical art and literature and the belief in the importance of individualism.

One of the key figures of this new movement was Italian artist Michelangelo, who is best known for his iconic works such as the Sistine Chapel ceiling and the statue of David. His use of realistic human anatomy and dramatic poses revolutionized the art world and inspired other artists to explore these techniques.

Another significant development during the Renaissance was the rise of oil painting. This medium allowed for more vibrant colors and finer details, and soon became the preferred method for many artists. Flemish painter Jan van Eyck is credited with perfecting the technique of oil painting, and his works are admired for their precision and lifelike qualities.

In addition to these advancements, the Renaissance also saw the emergence of famous artists such as Leonardo da Vinci, Raphael, and Titian. Their diverse styles and contributions further solidified the period as a golden age in the history of art.

Through the exploration of humanism, realism, and new mediums, the Renaissance period marked a significant shift in the world of art and continues to influence artists and art movements to this day.
