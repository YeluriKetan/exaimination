On this page, we will be discussing the concept of operant conditioning, a type of learning that occurs through rewards and punishments. According to psychologist B.F. Skinner, behavior can be shaped through consequences, specifically positive reinforcement and negative reinforcement. Positive reinforcement involves giving a reward, such as praise or a treat, after the desired behavior is performed. This increases the likelihood that the behavior will happen again in the future. On the other hand, negative reinforcement involves removing an unpleasant stimulus, such as loud noise or physical discomfort, after the desired behavior is performed. This also increases the likelihood of the behavior occurring again. It is important to note that both positive and negative reinforcement aim to increase the desired behavior, while punishment aims to decrease it. Punishment involves giving an unpleasant consequence, such as scolding or taking away a privilege, after an undesired behavior is displayed.
